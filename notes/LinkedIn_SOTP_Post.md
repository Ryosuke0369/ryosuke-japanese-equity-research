# LinkedIn Post — IHI SOTP Analysis

---

I built a Sum-of-the-Parts model for IHI Corporation (7013.T) — and the results reveal something the consolidated numbers completely hide.

𝗧𝗵𝗲 𝗣𝗿𝗼𝗯𝗹𝗲𝗺 𝘄𝗶𝘁𝗵 𝗖𝗼𝗻𝘀𝗼𝗹𝗶𝗱𝗮𝘁𝗲𝗱 𝗩𝗮𝗹𝘂𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻

When you value IHI on a consolidated basis — one WACC, one exit multiple — you're implicitly applying the same 15x EV/EBITDA to:

→ An aero engine business earning 22% operating margins
→ A social infrastructure business earning 0%

That's like averaging a Michelin-star restaurant with a cafeteria and calling them both "decent."

𝗪𝗵𝗮𝘁 𝗦𝗢𝗧𝗣 𝗥𝗲𝘃𝗲𝗮𝗹𝘀

When you break IHI into its four segments and apply peer-appropriate multiples:

• Aero, Space & Defense (OPM 22%) → 18x EV/EBITDA (Safran/MTU/RR median)
• Resources & Energy (OPM 6.5%) → 8x (energy equipment peers)
• Industrial Systems (OPM 5.7%) → 10x (turbocharger/machinery blend)
• Social Infrastructure (OPM ~0%) → 6x (infrastructure floor)

The result: 76% of IHI's enterprise value comes from a single segment.

The Aerospace & Defense business alone accounts for ¥2.7 trillion in EV — more than IHI's current market cap.

𝗧𝗵𝗲 𝗛𝗮𝗿𝗱𝗲𝘀𝘁 𝗣𝗮𝗿𝘁 𝗼𝗳 𝗦𝗢𝗧𝗣

It's not the math. It's the assumptions you don't realize you're making.

IHI doesn't disclose segment-level D&A. You have to estimate it. And the 3Q earnings presentation shows EBITDA composition of "Energy = 5%" — but that's a snapshot where 89% of Energy's full-year profit hasn't been recognized yet (Q4-heavy project completions).

If you blindly use that 5% figure, you understate Energy EBITDA by 4x.

The lesson: in SOTP, the quality of your EBITDA estimate matters more than the precision of your multiple selection.

𝗙𝗮𝗶𝗿 𝗩𝗮𝗹𝘂𝗲 𝗥𝗮𝗻𝗴𝗲

• Base case: ¥3,077/share (no conglomerate discount)
• Conservative: ¥2,739 (10% discount)
• Cross-check: sits between DCF-PGM (¥1,982) and DCF-Exit (¥3,463)

Full Excel model (6 sheets with sensitivity tables) and PDF report available on my GitHub.

This is my third valuation methodology for IHI, following the 12-page DCF/Comps report and LBO analysis. Building toward complete coverage as an independent equity researcher.
